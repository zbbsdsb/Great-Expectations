Sample runs

Output, not promises.

Each run follows the eight-section format. The first is a back-test that pinpoints exactly which completeness check Google Glass missed.

Input

Goal: Why did Google Glass fail, and what would GE have flagged pre-launch?

Consensus: Wearable AR diffuses like smartphones; enthusiasts preview the mass market.

Scarce: Social license — public trust about being recorded.

Output · 8 sections

1 — Assumption Inventory

CONSENSUS "breakthrough device sells on specs then crosses to mainstream" — false for socially-mediated wearables. SYSTEM-1 "camera-on-face is just a better phone" — ignored the other-person threat.

2 — First-Principles

Glasses = (FOV display) + (forward camera) + (social acceptability) + (power). Binding constraint is bystander consent, a non-engineering variable.

3 — Disruptive Pathways

A Consent-by-default: unmistakable recording indicator. B Enterprise-only beachhead (surgeons, warehouses) where being recorded is contractual.

4 — Completeness Mapping

① Bystander role never named → FAIL. ② Social license had no substitute → FAIL. ③ Stopped at 1st order → FAIL. ④ No Plan B at the irreversible first-impression node → FAIL.

5 — Stress-Test Report

All four tests FAIL on the original launch; Paths A/B pass. Original voided.

6 — Vulnerability

One viral "creepshot" (Non-linear shock) collapses the consumer thesis in 72h. Kill-switch: hard consent UI + enterprise pivot.

7 — Bias Audit

Overconfidence (engineering ≠ adoption); tech-centric bias (ignored the witnessed).

8 — Falsifiable

A visible indicator would cut ban incidents >50% in 6mo; enterprise-first hits $100M before any consumer try; no consent UI fixes the curve.

Input

Goal: Turn a SW-China agricultural county into a global Web3 content hub in 10 years.

Consensus: You need a tech metropolis + VC density + talent density first.

Scarce: Top Web3 talent willing to relocate to a farm county.

Output · 8 sections

1 — Assumption Inventory

CONSENSUS clusters require physical proximity to capital/talent; county competes on the same axes as Shenzhen → loses by definition.

2 — First-Principles

Hub = (attention) + (tooling) + (distribution) + (rails). None require a metropolis in a remote-first world.

3 — Disruptive Pathways

A Regulatory free port for creators (jurisdiction, not real estate). B Remote-first creator DAO — county as legal domicile, not office.

4 — Completeness Mapping

① Central gov gains monitoring KPI; farmers get rent expectations — named. ② Policy permission is binding; hedged. ③ Regulator ban modeled as 3rd-order shock. ④ Three options pre-bought at the notice node.

5 — Stress-Test Report

All four PASS. Paths survive the resource/policy/regulator stresses.

6 — Vulnerability

National blanket ban (Incomprehensible timing) freezes settlement. Kill-switch: pre-domiciled entity + fiat fallback.

7 — Bias Audit

Anchoring on "metropolis = innovation"; confirmation (crypto-bullish sources only).

8 — Falsifiable

>500 creators register yr1 without relocating; fiat fallback caps ban damage <30%; top-10 hub rank by yr5.

Input

Goal: Make a 2M-person city carbon-neutral by 2035.

Consensus: Electrify everything, build renewables, buy offsets for the rest.

Scarce: Grid stability during transition (intermittency + demand spikes).

Output · 8 sections

1 — Assumption Inventory

CONSENSUS more solar+wind+batteries solves it (ignores cement/steel/land); offsets are infinite/cheap/permanent.

2 — First-Principles

Emissions = (energy) + (materials) + (biosphere) + (waste). Only energy is electric. Binding constraint is materials + biosphere.

3 — Disruptive Pathways

A Circular by ordinance (low-carbon concrete, material passports). B Demand-flat city (cap floor-area, efficiency pricing).

4 — Completeness Mapping

① Construction margin sacrificed, named. ② Offsets not load-bearing → PASS. ③ Price-rise backlash + supply reshuffle modeled. ④ Three political-cover options pre-bought.

5 — Stress-Test Report

All four PASS.

6 — Vulnerability

Cold-snap + wind-lull blackout (Brittle). Kill-switch: dispatchable geothermal + demand response before any gas ban.

7 — Bias Audit

Optimism bias (offsets); single-axis (energy only).

8 — Falsifiable

Ordinance alone cuts built-environment emissions >40% by 2030; blackout risk <2% if geothermal ≥15% peak; floor-area cap holds demand <1%/yr.
