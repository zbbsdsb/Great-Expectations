# Example — Grand Plan: turn a SW-China agricultural county into a global Web3 content hub in 10 years

> Input goal: Transform a rural county into the world's leading Web3 content-creation center.
> Consensus: You need a tech metropolis, venture capital density, and talent density first.
> Scarcest resource: top-tier crypto/Web3 talent willing to relocate to a farm county.

## 1. Assumption Inventory
- `CONSENSUS` — "Innovation clusters require physical proximity to capital and talent." (Chanelled by assuming the county must import both.)
- `CONSENSUS` — "A county competes on the same axes as Shenzhen or Singapore." (Loses by definition.)
- `SYSTEM-1` — "Build infrastructure, they will come." (Linear extrapolation.)
- `ATOMIC` — Content creation = (attention) + (creator tooling) + (distribution) + (monetization rails). None require a physical metropolis in a remote-first world.

## 2. First-Principles Decomposition
A "content hub" is not buildings; it is a **sovereign attention economy**: creators anywhere + tokenized monetization + a jurisdiction that won't suddenly ban them. The county's *weakness* (no incumbents, cheap land, autonomous governance zone) is the lever.

## 3. Disruptive Pathways
- **Path A — "Regulatory free port for creators."** The county offers a special digital-asset sandbox + 0% creator tax + fast-track corporate registry. It competes on *jurisdiction*, not real estate. Breaks the value-proposition assumption that you must build a physical Silicon Valley.
- **Path B — "Remote-first creator DAO."** Fund 1,000 global creators with stipends tied to on-chain output; the county becomes the *legal domicile* and brand, not the office. Breaks the talent-relocation assumption entirely.

## 4. Completeness Mapping
- **① Stakeholder hologram:** Central government gains a monitoring KPI (capital flight risk); local farmers get displaced land-rent expectations; global creators gain a tax haven. **Who loses:** legacy real-estate brokers, the provincial tax base. Named explicitly.
- **② Resource antifragility:** Remove "top talent relocation" — Path B still runs (remote). Remove "policy permission" — both paths die. **So policy permission is the binding resource; pre-buy legal opinions + sunset clauses.**
- **③ 2nd / 3rd order:** 1st: creators register. 2nd: exchanges list the county's token; neighbors copy the model. 3rd: a national regulator, alarmed by capital flight, issues a blanket ban — the irreversible shock.
- **④ Spatiotemporal redundancy:** Most irreversible node = the moment a national regulator notices. Pre-buy three options: (B1) entity re-domicile to a friendly jurisdiction, (B2) token-less fiat settlement, (B3) pivot to "content education zone" branding.

## 5. Completeness Stress-Test Report
| Test | Verdict |
|------|---------|
| Stakeholder hologram | PASS |
| Resource antifragility | PASS (policy = binding; hedged) |
| 2nd/3rd-order | PASS (regulator shock modeled) |
| Spatiotemporal redundancy | PASS (3 options pre-bought) |

## 6. Vulnerability Stress Test (BANI)
Under *Brittle + Non-linear*: a single regulatory ban (Incomprehensible timing) freezes settlement. Kill-switch: pre-domiciled entity + fiat fallback so the creator economy survives without the token.

## 7. Cognitive Bias Audit
- **Anchoring** — on "metropolis = innovation." Path B breaks it.
- **Confirmation** — only reading crypto-bullish sources. Audit flags the need for a bear case.

## 8. Falsifiable Assertions
1. If the sandbox launches, >500 creators will register in year 1 without relocating.
2. A national blanket ban would cut settled value by <30% if the fiat fallback is live.
3. The county will reach global top-10 creator-hub rank by measured on-chain creator output by year 5.
