# Example — Grand Plan: make a mid-size city carbon-neutral by 2035

> Input goal: A 2M-person city reaches net-zero by 2035.
> Consensus: Electrify everything, build more renewables, buy offsets for the rest.
> Scarcest resource: grid stability during the transition (intermittency + demand spikes).

## 1. Assumption Inventory
- `CONSENSUS` — "More solar + wind + batteries solves it." (Ignores the non-energy emissions: cement, steel, land-use, waste.)
- `CONSENSUS` — "Offsets cover the hard-to-abate tail." (Treats offsets as infinite, cheap, permanent.)
- `SYSTEM-1` — "Technology will arrive in time." (Hope as a plan.)
- `ATOMIC` — Emissions = (energy) + (materials) + (land/biosphere) + (waste). Only the first is electric.

## 2. First-Principles Decomposition
"Carbon-neutral" is not "clean electrons." It is **closing four leak paths**: energy, materials, biosphere, waste. The binding constraint is materials (concrete/steel) and biosphere (urban tree cover loss), not the grid.

## 3. Disruptive Pathways
- **Path A — "Circular by ordinance."** Mandate low-carbon concrete + reused steel for all public builds; ban landfills via mandatory material passports. Breaks the assumption that decarbonization is an energy-only problem.
- **Path B — "Demand-flat city."** Cap per-capita floor-area and embed efficiency in building codes + pricing; reduce the denominator instead of only greening the numerator. Breaks the growth-implies-more-energy axiom.

## 4. Completeness Mapping
- **① Stakeholder hologram:** Cement/steel lobby loses volume (named); residents get a new comfort-standard KPI; waste haulers gain a sorting KPI. **Who is sacrificed:** short-term construction margin.
- **② Resource antifragility:** Remove "cheap offsets" (one resource class) — Path A/B still hit net-zero via real cuts. **Passes.** Remove "grid stability" — both stall; hedge with dispatchable geothermal + demand response.
- **③ 2nd / 3rd order:** 1st: emissions fall. 2nd: cement price rises → construction slows → political backlash. 3rd: a national building-materials shortage reshapes the policy; lock in domestic low-carbon supply.
- **④ Spatiotemporal redundancy:** Most irreversible node = the moment construction lobby secures a repeal. Pre-buy options: (B1) phased mandate, (B2) green-public-procurement-only, (B3) citizen carbon dividend to buy political cover.

## 5. Completeness Stress-Test Report
| Test | Verdict |
|------|---------|
| Stakeholder hologram | PASS |
| Resource antifragility | PASS (offsets not load-bearing) |
| 2nd/3rd-order | PASS (backlash modeled) |
| Spatiotemporal redundancy | PASS (3 options) |

## 6. Vulnerability Stress Test (BANI)
Under *Non-linear + Anxious*: a cold-snap + wind-lull (Brittle grid) triggers blackouts; blame flies to the plan. Kill-switch: dispatchable geothermal + automated demand response before any ban on gas.

## 7. Cognitive Bias Audit
- **Optimism bias** — assumed offsets remain cheap/available.
- **Single-axis** — fixated on energy, ignored materials/biosphere.

## 8. Falsifiable Assertions
1. With the circular-ordinance alone, built-environment emissions fall >40% by 2030.
2. A cold-snap blackout risk stays <2% if dispatchable geothermal ≥15% of peak.
3. Per-capita floor-area cap cuts total demand growth to <1%/yr despite population rise.
